Chicken Adobo

Chicken Adobo, also known as "Adobong Manok", is a widely known filipino cuisine and is also considered to be the national dish of Philippines. Although the spanish colonist were credited with giving the its name, filipinos have been preparing sour, vinegar-based dish since time was immemorial.

Ingredients

1 chicken (3 pounds), cut into pieces

2 tablespoon of vegetable oil

1 quartered and sliced large onion

A spoon of minced garlic

⅔ cup of soy sauce

⅓ cup of white vinegar

1 tablespoon of garlic powder

2 teaspoon of black pepper

1 bay leaf

Method

Step 1: In a large skillet, heat the vegetable oil on medium-high heat. Then proceed to cook the chicken pieces until golden brown (2-3 minutes each side). Transfer the chicken to set aside.

Step 2: Add the onions and garlic to the skillet, cook it about 3 to 5 minutes until its sofented and brown.

Step 3: Pour in the vinegar and soy sauce then season it garlic powder, black pepper, and bay leaf.

Step 4: Put the chicken back into the pan then increase to high, bring it to boil. Reduce heat to low, cover the skillet, and simmer for 35 to 40 minutes until the chicken is tender and cooked through.
